karbytes:

Is there a way to tell whether of not a specific phone number has blocked my phone number?

ChatGPT:

There is no direct or surefire way to confirm whether someone has blocked your phone number, as this information is generally not made available for privacy reasons. However, there are some signs that might suggest your number has been blocked:

1. Calls Go Straight to Voicemail: If your calls are consistently going straight to voicemail without ringing (or ring only once), this could indicate your number is blocked.

2. No Response to Messages: If you’re sending text messages and they are not being delivered or you don’t receive replies over a long period, it could be a sign of being blocked.

– On iPhones, if you’re sending iMessages and they remain blue (not delivered) or stay green after repeated attempts, this might indicate a block (although it could also be due to network issues).

– On Android, if your text messages say “sent” but not “delivered,” this could also be an indicator.

3. Busy Signal or Instant Disconnection: If you hear a busy signal or the call disconnects immediately after dialing, it could indicate a block.

4. Call Using Another Number: Try calling from a different phone number. If the person answers or the phone rings normally, it’s likely that your original number is blocked.

While these signs can indicate being blocked, they are not definitive proof, as network issues or the recipient having their phone turned off could cause similar behavior.

How long am I allowed to keep a Google Voice phone number?

You can keep a Google Voice phone number indefinitely, as long as you meet certain conditions to maintain the number. Google requires you to periodically use the number to prevent it from being reclaimed. Here are the key points:

1. Regular Usage: To keep your Google Voice number active, you need to use it. This means making a call, sending a text, or checking your voicemail at least once every six months.

2. Inactive Accounts: If you don’t use your Google Voice number for six months, Google may reclaim it, meaning you could lose access to the number. You’ll typically get warnings via email before this happens.

3. Number Transfer: You also have the option to port your Google Voice number to another service, which allows you to keep the number even if you no longer actively use Google Voice.

As long as you use the number regularly, you can keep it for as long as you like!
